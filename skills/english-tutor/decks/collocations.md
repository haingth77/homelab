# Collocations & Phrasal Verbs — Kết hợp từ tự nhiên

Collocations là cách người bản ngữ kết hợp từ tự nhiên. Sai collocation = nghe "lạ" dù ngữ pháp đúng.

## Make vs Do (Lỗi phổ biến của người Việt)

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ a decision
A: make a decision (make = tạo ra kết quả mới)

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ homework
A: do homework (do = thực hiện một nhiệm vụ)

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ a mistake
A: make a mistake

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ research
A: do research

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ progress
A: make progress

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ an effort
A: make an effort

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ damage
A: do damage

---

Q: "Make" or "Do"? ___ business
A: do business

---

Q: General rule for "make" vs "do"?
A: MAKE = create/produce something new (make a plan, make money, make a suggestion). DO = perform/complete an action (do work, do an experiment, do the dishes). Mẹo: MAKE → kết quả, DO → hành động.

## Verb + Noun Collocations (Academic)

Q: Collocation with "attention": pay ___ / draw ___ / attract ___
A: pay attention (to) / draw attention (to) / attract attention (KHÔNG nói "give attention to")

---

Q: Collocation with "role": play a ___ role
A: play a crucial/significant/vital/pivotal role (KHÔNG nói "do a role")

---

Q: Collocation with "impact": have an ___ on
A: have an impact/effect/influence on (KHÔNG nói "give impact")

---

Q: Collocation with "measures": take/implement/adopt ___
A: take measures / implement measures / adopt measures (to address/tackle a problem)

---

Q: Collocation with "trend": a ___ trend / ___ a trend
A: a growing/emerging/alarming trend / follow/buck/reverse a trend

---

C: The government [implemented] strict [measures] to [address] the housing crisis. (implement + measures + address — academic collocation chain)

---

C: This study [sheds light on] the relationship between screen time and sleep quality. (shed light on = illuminate/clarify)

---

Q: Collocation with "conclusion": reach/draw/come to a ___
A: reach a conclusion / draw a conclusion / come to a conclusion (KHÔNG nói "make a conclusion")

## Adjective + Noun Collocations

Q: "___ majority" — which adjective? (a very large majority)
A: a vast/overwhelming majority (KHÔNG nói "big majority")

---

Q: "___ difference" — which adjective? (a very big difference)
A: a significant/marked/stark/striking difference

---

Q: "___ attention" — which adjective? (careful attention)
A: close/careful/undivided attention

---

Q: "___ knowledge" — which adjective? (a lot of knowledge)
A: extensive/thorough/in-depth knowledge (KHÔNG nói "much knowledge" trong academic writing)

---

C: There is [mounting] evidence to suggest that early childhood education has [far-reaching] consequences. (mounting + evidence, far-reaching + consequences)

## Adverb + Adjective Collocations

Q: "___ aware" — which adverb? (very aware)
A: acutely/keenly/fully aware (Band 8: "I am acutely aware of the limitations.")

---

Q: "___ different" — which adverb? (very different)
A: vastly/fundamentally/markedly different

---

Q: "___ important" — which adverb? (very important)
A: critically/vitally/fundamentally important

---

C: The two approaches are [fundamentally] different in their [underlying] assumptions. (fundamentally + different, underlying + assumptions)

## Phrasal Verbs for IELTS

Q: What does "bring about" mean? Use in a sentence.
A: To cause something to happen. "Technology has brought about significant changes in education." (= cause/lead to — Band 7 alternative to "cause")

---

Q: What does "carry out" mean? Use in a sentence.
A: To perform/conduct. "The researchers carried out a comprehensive study." (= conduct — common in academic writing)

---

Q: What does "account for" mean? Use in a sentence.
A: To explain / to represent a proportion. "This factor alone accounts for 30% of the increase." (= explain hoặc chiếm tỷ lệ)

---

Q: What does "give rise to" mean? Use in a sentence.
A: To cause/produce. "Rapid urbanization has given rise to numerous social challenges." (= lead to — formal register)

---

Q: What does "rule out" mean? Use in a sentence.
A: To eliminate as a possibility. "We cannot rule out the possibility of hardware failure." (= loại trừ — SRE context!)

---

Q: What does "come up with" mean? Use in a sentence.
A: To think of/produce (an idea or plan). "The team came up with an innovative solution." (= devise/propose)

---

## Idiomatic Expressions (Band 7+ Speaking)

Q: What does "the tip of the iceberg" mean?
A: A small visible part of a much larger problem. "The reported incidents are just the tip of the iceberg." (Phần nổi của tảng băng chìm)

---

Q: What does "a double-edged sword" mean?
A: Something with both advantages and disadvantages. "Social media is a double-edged sword for teenagers." (Con dao hai lưỡi — rất hay cho Speaking Part 3)

---

Q: What does "from scratch" mean?
A: From the very beginning, with no prior preparation. "I built my homelab from scratch." (Từ đầu — familiar SRE context!)

---

Q: What does "the bottom line" mean?
A: The most important point/conclusion. "The bottom line is that we need more investment in education." (Điểm mấu chốt)

---

Q: What does "a steep learning curve" mean?
A: Difficult to learn initially. "Kubernetes has a steep learning curve." (Đường cong học tập dốc — SRE context)
