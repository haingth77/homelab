# Advanced Grammar — Ngữ pháp nâng cao

## Conditionals (Câu điều kiện)

Q: Complete the zero conditional: "If you ___ (heat) water to 100°C, it ___ (boil)."
A: If you heat water to 100°C, it boils. (Zero conditional: general truth — present simple both clauses)

---

Q: Complete the first conditional: "If the deployment ___ (fail), we ___ (trigger) a rollback."
A: If the deployment fails, we will trigger a rollback. (First conditional: real future possibility)

---

Q: Complete the second conditional: "If I ___ (have) more time, I ___ (learn) cinematography."
A: If I had more time, I would learn cinematography. (Second conditional: unreal present)

---

Q: Complete the third conditional: "If we ___ (test) the migration, we ___ (avoid) the outage."
A: If we had tested the migration, we would have avoided the outage. (Third conditional: unreal past)

---

Q: What is a mixed conditional? Give an example.
A: Combines different time frames. "If I had studied film (past), I would be a director now (present)." — past condition → present result

---

C: If I [were] you, I [would accept] the offer immediately. (Second conditional — subjunctive "were")

---

C: Had the team [been] notified earlier, the incident [would have been] resolved faster. (Inverted third conditional — formal/academic)

---

Q: Rewrite using inversion: "If I had known about the bug, I would have fixed it."
A: Had I known about the bug, I would have fixed it. (Inversion: remove "if", move auxiliary to front — Band 8 structure)

---

## Passive Voice (Câu bị động)

Q: Rewrite in passive: "The team deployed the update yesterday."
A: The update was deployed (by the team) yesterday. (Simple past passive: was/were + past participle)

---

Q: Rewrite in passive: "They are analyzing the data right now."
A: The data are being analyzed right now. (Present continuous passive: am/is/are + being + PP)

---

Q: Rewrite in passive: "Someone will review the code before release."
A: The code will be reviewed before release. (Future passive: will be + PP)

---

C: The results [were analyzed] using a mixed-methods [approach]. (Passive — academic writing standard)

---

C: It [is believed] that climate change [will accelerate] in the coming decades. (Impersonal passive — formal register)

---

Q: Rewrite using impersonal passive: "People think that AI will replace many jobs."
A: It is thought that AI will replace many jobs. / AI is thought to replace many jobs. (Impersonal passive — Band 8 academic structure)

---

## Relative Clauses (Mệnh đề quan hệ)

Q: Combine using a relative pronoun: "The server crashed. It was running the database."
A: The server which/that was running the database crashed. (Defining relative clause — essential information)

---

Q: Add a non-defining relative clause: "My manager approved the change. She has 15 years of experience."
A: My manager, who has 15 years of experience, approved the change. (Non-defining — extra info, uses commas)

---

C: The monitoring tool, [which] we implemented last month, [has significantly] reduced our response time. (Non-defining relative clause)

---

Q: What is a reduced relative clause? Give an example.
A: Omit the relative pronoun + be: "The report (which was) written by the team was thorough." → "The report written by the team was thorough." (Band 8 — concise academic style)

---

C: The engineer [responsible for] the deployment confirmed the fix. (Reduced relative: "who was responsible for" → "responsible for")

---

## Nominalization (Danh từ hóa)

Chuyển động từ/tính từ thành danh từ — kỹ năng quan trọng cho Writing Task 2 (Band 7+).

Q: Nominalize: "The climate changed rapidly."
A: The rapid change in climate... (verb "changed" → noun "change", adverb "rapidly" → adjective "rapid")

---

Q: Nominalize: "People consume too much energy."
A: The excessive consumption of energy... (verb "consume" → noun "consumption")

---

Q: Nominalize: "The government decided to increase taxes."
A: The government's decision to increase taxes... (verb "decided" → noun "decision")

---

Q: Nominalize: "Technology has developed significantly."
A: The significant development of technology... (verb "developed" → noun "development")

---

C: The [implementation] of stricter regulations has led to a [reduction] in emissions. (Nominalized: implement → implementation, reduce → reduction)

---

Q: Nominalize: "Students fail because they do not prepare."
A: Student failure is attributed to a lack of preparation. (Both clauses nominalized — full academic register)

---

## Cleft Sentences (Câu chẻ)

Q: Rewrite as a cleft sentence for emphasis: "The network latency caused the timeout."
A: It was the network latency that caused the timeout. (It-cleft: emphasizes the subject)

---

Q: Rewrite as a cleft sentence: "I admire her resilience most."
A: What I admire most is her resilience. (What-cleft: emphasizes the object)

---

C: It [is] the lack of investment [that] has led to infrastructure decay. (It-cleft — Band 8 emphasis structure)

---

## Inversion (Đảo ngữ)

Q: Rewrite using inversion: "I have never seen such a beautiful film."
A: Never have I seen such a beautiful film. (Negative adverb inversion — formal/literary)

---

Q: Rewrite using inversion: "The team not only fixed the bug but also improved performance."
A: Not only did the team fix the bug, but they also improved performance. (Not only...but also inversion)

---

C: Rarely [does] one encounter such a compelling argument in academic literature. (Negative adverb inversion — Band 8)

---

C: Only after the investigation [was] completed [did] the full extent of the damage become clear. (Only after inversion)

---

## Subject-Verb Agreement (Hòa hợp chủ-vị)

Q: Correct the error: "The number of incidents have decreased."
A: The number of incidents has decreased. ("The number of" → singular verb; "A number of" → plural verb)

---

Q: Correct: "Neither the manager nor the engineers was available."
A: Neither the manager nor the engineers were available. (Neither...nor: verb agrees with the nearest subject)

---

C: Each of the participants [was] given a unique identifier. (Each + singular verb)

---

C: The data [suggest] that the hypothesis is correct. ("Data" is plural in academic English)

---

## Articles (Mạo từ)

Tiếng Việt không có mạo từ — đây là lỗi phổ biến nhất của người Việt học tiếng Anh.

Q: Fill in: "___ unemployment is a major concern in ___ developing countries."
A: (no article) Unemployment is a major concern in (no article) developing countries. (General/uncountable concepts — no article)

---

Q: Fill in: "___ Internet has transformed ___ way we communicate."
A: The Internet has transformed the way we communicate. ("The" = specific, known reference)

---

Q: When do we use "a/an" vs "the"?
A: "a/an" = first mention, one of many; "the" = already known, specific, unique. Quy tắc: lần đầu nhắc → a/an, lần sau nhắc → the.

---

C: [The] government introduced [a] new policy to address [the] rising cost of living. (the = specific government; a = one new policy; the = specific cost)

---

Q: Which is correct? "She has experience / an experience / the experience in project management."
A: "She has experience in project management." (Uncountable abstract concept — no article. "An experience" = một trải nghiệm cụ thể)

---

## Tense Consistency (Nhất quán thì)

Q: Fix the tense error: "The study showed that climate change is accelerated by human activity."
A: Correct as-is if referring to a general truth. OR: "...was accelerated..." if reporting past findings only. (General truths keep present tense even in reported speech)

---

Q: Which tense for academic writing conclusions?
A: Present simple for general findings: "The data suggest..." Past simple for specific research actions: "The study found..." (Academic convention)

---

C: The researchers [conducted] the experiment in 2024 and [concluded] that the results [support] the initial hypothesis. (Past for actions, present for ongoing validity)
