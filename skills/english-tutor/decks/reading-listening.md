# Reading & Listening Strategies — Chiến lược đọc và nghe IELTS

## Reading — General Strategies

Q: What are the 3 reading techniques for IELTS?
A: 1) Skimming — read quickly for main idea (30s per passage) 2) Scanning — search for specific information (names, dates, numbers) 3) Detailed reading — read carefully for meaning and inference. Dùng cả 3 theo thứ tự.

---

Q: How should you approach a reading passage? (Step by step)
A: 1) Read the title and first sentence of each paragraph (1 min) 2) Read questions first to know what to look for 3) Locate relevant paragraphs 4) Read those paragraphs in detail 5) Answer. KHÔNG đọc toàn bộ passage trước.

---

Q: What is the #1 IELTS reading trap?
A: Paraphrase — questions NEVER use the same words as the passage. "Rapid increase" in the passage = "sharp rise" in the question. Always look for synonyms and reworded ideas.

---

## True / False / Not Given (T/F/NG)

Q: Explain the difference between FALSE and NOT GIVEN.
A: FALSE = the passage says the OPPOSITE. NOT GIVEN = the passage doesn't mention this at all. Mẹo: nếu bạn phải suy đoán, có thể là NG.

---

Q: Strategy for T/F/NG questions?
A: 1) Read the statement carefully 2) Find the relevant part of the passage 3) Compare EXACTLY. TRUE = same meaning, different words. FALSE = contradicts. NG = no information either way. KHÔNG dùng kiến thức ngoài bài.

---

Q: Passage says: "The population of Tokyo exceeds 13 million." Statement: "Tokyo has the largest population in Japan." T/F/NG?
A: NOT GIVEN — the passage only states Tokyo's population, not that it's the largest. (Trap: "exceeds 13 million" doesn't mean "largest")

---

Q: Passage says: "Renewable energy sources accounted for 20% of total energy production." Statement: "Most energy production came from renewable sources." T/F/NG?
A: FALSE — 20% is not "most" (which implies >50%). The passage directly contradicts the statement.

---

## Matching Headings

Q: Strategy for matching headings to paragraphs?
A: 1) Read all headings first 2) Identify the main idea of each paragraph (usually in the first or last sentence) 3) Match based on MAIN idea, not details 4) Cross out headings as you use them. Trap: headings that match a detail, not the main idea.

---

Q: What's the difference between a main idea and a detail?
A: Main idea = what the entire paragraph is ABOUT. Detail = a specific fact or example WITHIN the paragraph. Heading must match the main idea. Ví dụ: paragraph about "causes of deforestation" with examples of logging and agriculture — heading = "Causes" not "Logging."

---

## Sentence Completion

Q: Strategy for sentence completion questions?
A: 1) Read the incomplete sentence 2) Predict the type of word needed (noun? verb? number?) 3) Locate the relevant part of the passage 4) Use the EXACT words from the passage (unless paraphrase is required) 5) Check word limit. Đếm số từ kỹ.

---

## Summary Completion

Q: Strategy for summary completion?
A: 1) Read the summary to understand the overall topic 2) Identify which part of the passage it covers 3) Fill gaps with words from the passage or the word list 4) Check grammar — the completed sentence must be grammatically correct.

---

## Reading — Inference Skills

Q: What is an inference question?
A: The answer is NOT directly stated — you must deduce it from clues in the text. Signal words: "implies," "suggests," "can be inferred." Suy luận từ bằng chứng, không phải đoán.

---

Q: Passage: "Despite receiving critical acclaim, the film failed to recoup its production costs." What can be inferred?
A: The film was well-reviewed by critics but was not commercially successful. (Critical acclaim = good reviews; failed to recoup costs = didn't make enough money)

---

Q: Passage: "The government allocated 15% of GDP to education, up from 8% a decade ago." What can be inferred?
A: Education has become a higher priority for the government over the past decade. (Spending nearly doubled → increased prioritization)

## Listening — General Strategies

Q: What are the 4 sections of IELTS Listening?
A: Section 1: Social conversation (2 speakers) — easiest. Section 2: Social monologue (1 speaker). Section 3: Academic discussion (2-3 speakers). Section 4: Academic lecture (1 speaker) — hardest.

---

Q: What should you do before the audio plays?
A: READ the questions! Use the 30-second preview time to: 1) Underline keywords 2) Predict answer types (name? number? noun?) 3) Note any traps (negatives, qualifiers). Không đọc trước = mất điểm.

---

Q: What is the dictation method for practice?
A: 1) Listen to a section 2) Transcribe every word 3) Compare with transcript 4) Identify words/phrases you missed 5) Practice those specific sounds. Rất hiệu quả cho connected speech.

---

## Listening — Connected Speech Traps

Q: What is connected speech?
A: How words blend together in natural English. "Would have" → /wʊdəv/; "Did you" → /dɪdʒuː/; "Going to" → /gənə/. Understanding this is critical for Sections 3-4.

---

Q: What are common connected speech patterns?
A: 1) Linking (consonant → vowel): "turn_off" 2) Elision (dropped sounds): "nex(t) week" 3) Assimilation (sound changes): "ten bikes" → /tem baɪks/ 4) Weak forms: "can" → /kən/, "was" → /wəz/

---

Q: What is a listening "distractor"?
A: When the speaker mentions an answer then CHANGES it. "The meeting is on Tuesday... actually, no, it's been moved to Wednesday." Answer = Wednesday. Always listen for corrections: "actually," "I mean," "sorry, I meant..."

---

## Listening — Note/Form Completion

Q: Strategy for form/note completion?
A: 1) Read the form to predict answer types (name, date, number, place) 2) Listen for spelling (they often spell names) 3) Write numbers as you hear them 4) Check word limits. Spelling counts!

---

Q: What types of answers are tested in note completion?
A: Names (spelled out), numbers (phone, dates, amounts), addresses, times, single nouns. Mẹo: nếu đáp án là số, viết số; nếu là tên, chú ý spelling.

---

## Listening — Map/Diagram Labeling

Q: Strategy for map labeling questions?
A: 1) Study the map during preview time 2) Identify the starting point and orientation 3) Listen for direction words: "opposite," "next to," "between," "on the corner of" 4) Follow the speaker's route mentally.

---

## Paraphrase Recognition (Critical Skill)

Q: Match the paraphrase: Passage says "a marked increase" → Question says ___?
A: "a significant/sharp/notable rise" — all synonyms for "marked increase"

---

Q: Match: "the primary factor" → ___?
A: "the main/chief/principal reason" — synonyms for "primary factor"

---

Q: Match: "pose a threat to" → ___?
A: "endanger / jeopardize / put at risk" — all mean "pose a threat to"

---

Q: Match: "in the vicinity of" → ___?
A: "near / close to / in proximity to" — spatial paraphrase

---

Q: Match: "a substantial proportion" → ___?
A: "a significant/considerable/large percentage" — quantity paraphrase

## Time Management

Q: How should you allocate time in IELTS Reading? (60 minutes, 3 passages)
A: Passage 1: 15 min (easiest). Passage 2: 20 min (medium). Passage 3: 25 min (hardest). If stuck on one question for >2 min, guess and move on.

---

Q: How should you use the 10-minute transfer time in Listening?
A: Transfer answers to the answer sheet AND check: spelling, word limits, grammar agreement (singular/plural), capitalization of proper nouns.
