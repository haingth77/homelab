# Academic Vocabulary — Từ vựng học thuật

Từ vựng cấp Band 7-8 thay thế cho những từ thông dụng. Mỗi thẻ cho từ thông dụng (Band 5-6) và từ nâng cao (Band 7-8).

## Band Upgrades — Common → Academic

Q: Band 8 replacement for "very important"?
A: crucial / paramount / pivotal (Ví dụ: "Education is pivotal to economic growth.")

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "very good"?
A: exceptional / outstanding / exemplary (Ví dụ: "The results were exemplary.")

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "very bad"?
A: detrimental / catastrophic / devastating (Ví dụ: "Pollution has a detrimental impact on health.")

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "a lot of"?
A: a considerable/substantial number of (countable) / a significant amount of (uncountable)

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "more and more"?
A: an increasing number of / increasingly / a growing proportion of

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "show" (in academic writing)?
A: demonstrate / illustrate / indicate / reveal (Ví dụ: "The graph illustrates a steady decline.")

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "think"?
A: contend / maintain / assert / argue (Ví dụ: "Some scholars contend that...")

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "because"?
A: owing to / due to / as a consequence of / on account of (Ví dụ: "Owing to rapid urbanization,...")

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "but"?
A: nevertheless / however / conversely / notwithstanding / on the other hand

---

Q: Band 8 replacement for "also"?
A: furthermore / moreover / in addition / additionally / likewise

---

## Academic Word List (AWL) Essentials

Q: Define "paradigm" and use it in a sentence.
A: A model or pattern. "The shift to remote work represents a paradigm change in employment." (Mô hình — thường dùng với "shift" hoặc "change")

---

Q: Define "mitigate" and use it in a sentence.
A: To make less severe. "Governments must mitigate the effects of climate change." (Giảm nhẹ — khác "eliminate" vì không xóa bỏ hoàn toàn)

---

Q: Define "exacerbate" and use it in a sentence.
A: To make worse. "Poor infrastructure exacerbates the impact of natural disasters." (Làm trầm trọng hơn — đối lập với "mitigate")

---

Q: Define "unprecedented" and use it in a sentence.
A: Never done or known before. "The pandemic caused unprecedented disruption to global supply chains." (Chưa từng có tiền lệ)

---

Q: Define "proliferation" and use it in a sentence.
A: Rapid increase in number. "The proliferation of social media has transformed communication." (Sự phổ biến/gia tăng nhanh chóng)

---

Q: Define "inherent" and use it in a sentence.
A: Existing as a natural or permanent quality. "There are inherent risks in any large-scale deployment." (Vốn có, cố hữu)

---

Q: Define "ubiquitous" and use it in a sentence.
A: Present everywhere. "Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society." (Có mặt khắp nơi)

---

Q: Define "pragmatic" and use it in a sentence.
A: Dealing with things practically. "A pragmatic approach to infrastructure scaling is preferred." (Thực dụng, thiết thực)

---

Q: Define "autonomous" and use it in a sentence.
A: Independent, self-governing. "Autonomous vehicles are expected to reduce traffic accidents." (Tự chủ, tự động)

---

Q: Define "juxtapose" and use it in a sentence.
A: Place side by side for comparison. "The director juxtaposed urban decay with natural beauty." (Đặt cạnh nhau để so sánh — từ hay cho filmmaking topics)

## Hedging Language (Ngôn ngữ thận trọng)

Quan trọng cho Band 7+ — tránh nói quá tuyệt đối trong academic writing.

Q: What is hedging and why is it important for IELTS?
A: Using cautious language to avoid absolute claims. Band 7-8 writers hedge: "It could be argued..." instead of "It is obvious..." (Thể hiện tư duy phản biện)

---

Q: Hedge this: "Social media causes depression in teenagers."
A: "Social media may contribute to depression among some teenagers." (may/might/could + contribute to/be linked to — không nói "causes" tuyệt đối)

---

C: It [could be argued] that technology has [somewhat] diminished face-to-face interaction. (Hedged: could be argued + somewhat)

---

Q: List 5 hedging expressions for academic writing.
A: 1) It could be argued that... 2) Evidence suggests that... 3) This may/might lead to... 4) It is widely believed that... 5) To some extent,...

---

C: The findings [tend to suggest] that early intervention [is likely to] produce better outcomes. (Double hedging: tend to suggest + is likely to)

---

## Formal vs Informal Register

Q: Formalize: "Kids these days spend too much time on their phones."
A: "Young people in contemporary society arguably devote an excessive amount of time to mobile devices." (Academic register upgrade)

---

Q: Formalize: "The government should do something about pollution."
A: "It is imperative that the government implement measures to address environmental pollution." (Subjunctive "implement" + formal phrasing)

---

Q: Formalize: "Lots of people don't agree with this idea."
A: "A significant proportion of the population opposes this notion." (Quantifier upgrade + formal verb)

---

C: The [aforementioned] study [yielded] results that [corroborate] the initial hypothesis. (Formal: aforementioned, yielded, corroborate)

## Precise Synonyms — Avoid Repetition

Q: Give 5 synonyms for "increase" (noun/verb).
A: rise, surge, escalation, growth, expansion (noun) / escalate, soar, spike, climb, amplify (verb)

---

Q: Give 5 synonyms for "decrease" (noun/verb).
A: decline, reduction, drop, downturn, contraction (noun) / diminish, plummet, dwindle, wane, contract (verb)

---

Q: Give 5 synonyms for "problem."
A: issue, challenge, obstacle, impediment, predicament (Mỗi từ có sắc thái khác: issue = neutral, predicament = serious situation)

---

Q: Give 5 synonyms for "advantage."
A: benefit, merit, asset, strength, virtue (Ví dụ: "One notable merit of this approach is...")

---

Q: Give 5 synonyms for "disadvantage."
A: drawback, shortcoming, limitation, downside, pitfall

---

Q: Distinguish: "affect" vs "effect."
A: "affect" = verb (to influence); "effect" = noun (result) OR verb (to bring about). "The policy affected millions." / "The effect was significant." / "The government effected change."
